# GF.sh — Skills4People / The AI Scientist
## DEMO VIDEO SCRIPT  |  2 minutes  |  Audience: scientists, chemists, engineers, medicine MSc

---

### [0:00 – 0:18]  HOOK  *(voice-over, no screen)*

> "You're a researcher. Chemist, mechanical engineer, medicine MSc — it doesn't matter. You have a hypothesis. You have the scientific understanding to judge whether a protocol makes sense. But before you run a single experiment, you're spending days — sometimes a full week — writing protocols, hunting supplier catalogs, building budgets, checking safety regulations.
>
> That's not the hard part of science. That's just administration. **We built a tool to remove it.**"

---

### [0:18 – 0:32]  INPUT  *(screen: hypothesis form)*

> "Open Skills4People. Pick your experiment category — Cell Biology. Name the experiment. Then type your hypothesis in plain language, exactly as you'd write it in your notebook."

*[Type]:*
> *"Replacing DMSO with trehalose as a cryoprotectant will increase post-thaw viability of HeLa cells by at least 15 percentage points, due to trehalose's superior membrane stabilisation at low temperatures."*

> "Hit run. That's the entire input."

---

### [0:32 – 0:50]  LITERATURE QC  *(screen: QC result)*

> "In seconds — not hours — the system searches six academic databases simultaneously. Semantic Scholar, PubMed, arXiv, OpenAlex, protocol repositories.
>
> Result: *'Similar work exists'* — two directly relevant references, ranked by overlap with your exact hypothesis. Not a generic search. Grounded to your question.
>
> You didn't open a single browser tab."

---

### [0:50 – 1:15]  PLAN + CRITIQUE  *(screen: plan dashboard, critique panel)*

> "The full experiment plan appears. Step-by-step protocol grounded in published research. Every reagent with a catalog number — Sigma-Aldrich, ATCC. Line-item budget. Phased timeline with decision gates. A complete validation design including statistical analysis plan.
>
> **But then — this.**
>
> The AI immediately critiques its own plan. It scans for six categories of weakness: missing controls, insufficient sample size, validation gaps, safety oversights, feasibility issues, statistical design problems.
>
> Right here it flags: *'No vehicle control specified for the DMSO comparison group'* and *'n=3 replicates insufficient for the stated 15-point threshold — recommend n=6 based on expected variance in HeLa viability assays.'*
>
> As a cell biologist, you already know both of those are real issues. The AI caught them in the same breath it generated the plan — before you spent a cent."

---

### [1:15 – 1:35]  DOCUMENT UPLOAD  *(screen: settings / document upload)*

> "Now — your lab has its own SOPs. Standard freeze-thaw protocols, internal safety guidelines, your preferred suppliers. Upload them. PDF, plain text, it doesn't matter.
>
> You can attach documents at the organisation level — so every plan your team generates inherits your lab's standards — or at the individual experiment level for experiment-specific context.
>
> Hit Regenerate. The new plan now reflects your own protocols, not just the literature."

---

### [1:35 – 1:50]  FEEDBACK LOOP  *(screen: feedback modal + applied rules panel)*

> "The system suggested n=6. You know from your lab's data that n=4 is sufficient for this specific cell line. Click *Suggest Correction*, type your reasoning.
>
> The system asks: save this for just this experiment, for all Cell Biology experiments, or for your entire organisation? You choose Category.
>
> Next time anyone on your team runs a cryo experiment, that rule is automatically injected into the plan — labelled, visible, applied. **The platform learns from your expertise.**"

---

### [1:50 – 2:00]  CLOSE  *(voice-over)*

> "We're researchers. We know what it costs to lose a week before the real work starts. Skills4People gives that time back — and gets sharper every time a scientist uses it.
>
> Every plan saved. Every correction made. Every document uploaded. The next plan is better.
>
> **GF.sh. Tools for people who actually do the science.**"
